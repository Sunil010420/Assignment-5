## Overview

Meeting scheduling in Microsoft Teams allows you to plan video, audio, or hybrid meetings in advance and invite participants.

When you schedule a meeting, Teams creates a calendar event, generates a meeting join link, and sends invitations to attendees.

Attendees can accept, decline, or propose a new meeting time, and Teams automatically adds the meeting to their calendars.

If your Teams account is connected to Outlook, meetings you schedule in Teams appear in Outlook, and meetings you schedule in Outlook appear in Teams.

### Meeting Scheduling Options

Choose a scheduled meeting instead of an instant **Meet now** call when you need to:

- Notify attendees in advance.
- Schedule recurring meetings, such as weekly stand-ups.
- Add an agenda, channel, or files before the meeting starts.
- Help attendees in different time zones receive calendar invitations and reminders.
  
Microsoft Teams lets you schedule meetings in two ways:

- **Calendar:** Schedule one-time or recurring meetings with any attendees from the Teams calendar.
- **Channel:** Schedule a meeting from a channel to post it in the channel conversation and make it visible to all channel members.

Teams syncs scheduled meetings with Outlook when you connect your account.

## Schedule a meeting in Teams

Schedule a meeting from the Teams calendar by following this process.

### Before you begin
- Sign in to Teams.
- Have the email addresses or Teams contact names of the attendees.
- Ensure you have a stable internet connection.
  
To schedule a meeting

1. In the left navigation pane, select **Calendar**.
2. Select **New meeting**.
3. In the **Title** field, enter a meeting name.
4. Add attendees:
   - In the **Add required attendees** field, enter the name or email address of each required attendee.
   - Select **Optional** to add attendees whose attendance is optional.
5. Set the meeting date and time:
   - Select the start date and time.
   - Select the end date and time.
6. (Optional) Select a **Channel** to post the meeting in a team channel. Leave the field blank to create a private meeting.
7. In the **Details** field, enter an agenda or meeting notes.
8. Select <kbd>Save</kbd>.

**Note:** You can edit or cancel the meeting from **Calendar** before the scheduled meeting time.

Teams sends meeting invitations to the attendees and adds the meeting to your calendar.

## Meeting options reference

Use meeting options to control who can join, present, and speak in a scheduled meeting.
Open meeting options from the meeting invitation by selecting **Meeting options**.

### Meeting options

| Option | Description | Default value |
|:--------|:------:|------:|
| Who can bypass the lobby. | Sets which attendees join the meeting directly instead of waiting in the lobby. | People in your organization. |
| Always let callers bypass the lobby. | Allows anyone who dials in by phone to skip the lobby. | Off |
| Announce when callers join or leave. | Plays an audio announcement when a phone participant joins or leaves. | On |
| Choose co-organizers. | Assigns co-organizer permissions to selected attendees. | None |
| Who can present. | Sets which attendees can share content during the meeting. | Everyone |
| Allow mic for attendees. | Lets attendees unmute their microphone. | On |
| Allow camera for attendees. | Lets attendees turn on their camera. | On |
| Allow meeting chat. | Sets whether chat is enabled during the meeting. | Enabled |
| Allow reactions. | Lets attendees send reactions during the meeting. | On |

### Keyboard shortcuts

| Action | Shortcut |
|--------|------|
| Accept a meeting invite | Ctrl+Enter |
| Toggle mute | Ctrl+Shift+M |
| Toggle camera | Ctrl+Shift+O |
| Show meeting chat | Ctrl+Shift+C |
| Leave a meeting | Ctrl+Shift+H |

### Best Practices

- Send invites at least 24 hours in advance to give attendees time to check availability and prepare.
- *Verify the time zone* before sending, especially when attendees are in different regions.
- *Avoid scheduling meetings during common non-working hours* unless necessary, to respect attendee availability.
